Government Solar Initiatives in India

Explore India's commitment to solar energy for a sustainable future!

National Solar Mission (JNNSM)

Launched: 2010

Objective: Achieve 100 GW by 2022, targeting 280 GW by 2030. Promotes grid-connected solar plants and rooftop systems.

  • Encourages rooftop solar for all sectors.
  • Framework for state-level solar adoption.
  • Aims for grid parity and tariff reduction.

Solar Rooftop Scheme

Implemented by: MNRE

  • Up to 40% subsidy for residential systems up to 3 kW.
  • 20% subsidy for systems between 3-10 kW.
  • Net metering allows selling excess energy back to the grid.

KUSUM Scheme

Launched: 2019

  • Component A: Grid-connected solar plants up to 2 MW by farmers.
  • Component B: Off-grid solar water pumps.
  • Component C: Solarization of grid-connected pumps.
  • 60% of the cost is subsidized by the government.

Solar Parks & Ultra Mega Solar Projects

Implemented by: MNRE

  • Government assists with land acquisition and infrastructure.
  • Target: Develop 50 Solar Parks with 40 GW capacity.
  • Key states: Rajasthan, Gujarat, MP, Karnataka, AP.

PM-KUSUM

Launched: 2019

  • Component A: Solar plants (up to 2 MW) on farmers' barren land.
  • Subsidies for standalone solar pumps for irrigation.
  • Solarization of existing grid-connected pumps.

Development of Solar Cities

Objective: Reduce conventional energy dependency by 10% through renewable energy.

  • Target: Develop 60 solar cities.
  • Promote solar technology in public and private sectors.
  • Provide funding support for solar projects.

Atal Jyoti Yojana (AJAY)

Launched by: MNRE

  • Install solar-powered streetlights in rural and semi-urban areas.
  • Focus on districts with low electricity access.
  • Emphasis on backward districts and border areas.

Solar Manufacturing & ‘Make in India’

Objective: Boost domestic manufacturing of solar equipment, reducing imports.

  • Production Linked Incentive (PLI) for solar PV manufacturing.
  • Custom duties on imported solar panels.
  • Solar SEZs with tax incentives.

Green Energy Corridor

Objective: Develop a dedicated transmission network for renewable energy.

  • Funded by the Indian government and international agencies.
  • Integrate large-scale renewable energy into the national grid.

International Solar Alliance (ISA)

Launched: 2015

  • India leads global solar energy efforts, especially in tropical countries.
  • Promotes solar adoption and cost reduction globally.
