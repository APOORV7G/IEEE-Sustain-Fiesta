Affordable and Clean Energy : SDG - 7

Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all.

Why It Matters

Access to energy is a critical enabler of business, education, and healthcare. However, 789 million people around the world still lack access to electricity.

Investing in renewable energy, energy efficiency, and sustainable energy solutions is key to combating climate change and providing clean energy for all.

 

789 million people

Lack access to electricity, primarily in sub-Saharan Africa.

17% Share

Of renewables in total energy consumption as of 2017.

$21.4 billion

Financial flows to developing countries for renewable energy in 2017.

Decent Work and Economic Growth : SDG - 8

Promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all.

Why It Matters

Sustained and inclusive economic growth can drive progress, create decent jobs for all, and improve living standards.

Investing in education, skills training, and sustainable employment solutions is essential to ensure decent work opportunities for all and to foster economic recovery.

 

1.6 Billion Workers

Workers in the informal economy risked losing their livelihoods during the pandemic.

22% Youth Unemployment

In 2019, 22% of the world's youth were not engaged in education, employment, or training.

400 Million Job Losses

Estimated number of full-time job losses due to COVID-19 in the second quarter of 2020.

Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure: SDG - 9

Build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and foster innovation.

Why It Matters

Infrastructure, industrialization, and innovation are critical to driving economic growth, reducing inequality, and advancing climate action.

Without sustainable industrialization and resilient infrastructure, achieving economic progress and improving living standards becomes increasingly challenging, particularly in developing countries.

 

51% Decline in Air Passengers

Air passenger numbers fell by 51% from January to May 2020 due to the pandemic.

35% Credit Access

Only 35% of small-scale industries in developing countries have access to credit (2006-2018).

54% Global Internet Access

As of 2019, only 54% of the global population uses the internet, with just 19% in least developed countries.

Sustainable Cities and Communities: SDG - 11

Make cities inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.

Why It Matters

Over 90% of COVID-19 cases have occurred in urban areas, exposing the vulnerabilities of densely populated cities. Urban centers also face challenges such as air pollution, inadequate infrastructure, and unplanned growth.

Creating inclusive, safe, and resilient cities is crucial to ensure a better quality of life, reduce inequality, and combat climate change.

 

24% Urban Slum Population

As of 2018, 24% of the global urban population lived in slums.

90% of COVID-19 Cases in Urban Areas

Over 90% of COVID-19 cases have occurred in urban areas, highlighting their vulnerabilities.

47% Proximity to Public Spaces

Only 47% of the global urban population live within 400 meters of an open public space.

Climate Action: SDG - 13

Take urgent action to tackle climate change and its impacts.

Why It Matters

The climate crisis is intensifying, with 2010-2019 being the warmest decade ever recorded. Climate change is disrupting national economies, affecting lives, and exacerbating natural disasters such as wildfires, floods, and hurricanes.

We must limit global warming to 1.5°C to prevent further irreversible damage. Urgent action is required to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and build climate resilience.

 

7.6% Annual Reduction

To limit global warming to 1.5°C, greenhouse gas emissions must fall by 7.6% every year starting in 2020.

39 Million People Affected

In 2018, more than 39 million people were affected by climate-related disasters.

$781 Billion Fossil Fuel Investment

Investment in fossil fuels continues to exceed investment in climate activities, with $781 billion allocated in 2016 alone.
